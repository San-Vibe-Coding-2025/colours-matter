What is Color Vision Deficiency (CVD)?

Color Vision Deficiency, commonly called "color blindness," affects how people perceive colors. It's a genetic condition inherited from one's mother in most cases, though it can also be acquired due to aging, diseases like diabetes and multiple sclerosis, or from medications.

The biggest myth: People with CVD don't just confuse red with green. The condition affects perception across the entire color spectrum, potentially impacting reds, greens, oranges, browns, purples, pinks, and grays. Even black can be confused with dark red, green, or blue.

Most people with moderate red/green color blindness can only accurately identify about 5 colored pencils from a standard box of 24 crayons, compared to the full 24 that people with normal vision can distinguish.

What is Cividis?

Cividis is a scientifically-designed color palette specifically created to be completely accessible to people with color vision deficiency. Unlike traditional color schemes, Cividis was mathematically engineered to avoid problematic green colors that cause confusion for the most common types of colorblindness.

Why Cividis over other palettes? While Viridis is perceptually uniform, Cividis takes accessibility one step further by completely eliminating the green spectrum that causes issues for deuteranopia and protanopia (affecting 8% of men). This makes it the gold standard for truly inclusive design.

Why We Built This

300 million people worldwide have color vision deficiency. That's nearly the entire population of the United States. Yet the vast majority of websites, applications, and digital tools are designed without considering their needs.

We built the Cividis Theme Engine because accessibility shouldn't be an afterthought. Every person deserves equal access to information, regardless of how they perceive color.

Our Goals:

  • • Make websites automatically accessible to people with CVD
  • • Eliminate the need for manual color accessibility audits
  • • Provide beautiful, scientifically-backed color schemes
  • • Raise awareness about color vision deficiency
  • • Prove that inclusive design benefits everyone

With just one line of code, developers can transform their websites to be inclusive of all users. The Cividis palette isn't just accessible—it's beautiful, creating harmonious color relationships that enhance the user experience for everyone, while being completely free of problematic green tones.
